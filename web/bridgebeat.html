A WORKING BRIDGE-PHYSICS ENGINE — NOT A SLIDER TOY

We simulate how a bridge bends under traffic from first principles — then run the physics backwards to weigh the trucks and read the bridge's health.

Every line you'll see is computed from the equations of structural dynamics, not drawn by hand. A truck crosses → a finite-element model of the deck computes the bending → we invert that bending to recover the truck's weight (Bridge Weigh-in-Motion). The same vibration reveals damage, and on a real bridge (KW51, Belgium) it tracks 16 months of health. Five acts below — and the Math switch (top-right) opens the back of the watch: the real formulas and live numbers doing the work.
