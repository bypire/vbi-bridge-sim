Turn any car into a bridge inspector

A passing vehicle's vibration reveals the bridge's natural frequency. Damage the deck and the frequency drops, detected from the car, no sensors on the bridge. Deflection magnified —.

How: a quarter-car crosses at a slow scanning speed; we reconstruct the tyre contact-point response from its accelerations (Yang 2020), and read the bridge's first natural frequency from the spectrum. A local stiffness loss (EI) over the deck's centre lowers that frequency.

Honest scope: smooth road shown here, this is the clean regime. Real road roughness buries the peak and needs two-axle / residual techniques; frequency is also a blunt, global damage indicator (small local damage barely shifts it). Verified end-to-end against FEM ground truth.
